KODAIKANAL


PLACES :

    PINE FOREST

  • PINE FOREST

    • Located in the southern part of Kodaikanal, this forest is a haven for nature lovers and a perfect escape from the hustle and bustle of city life. As you step into this forest, you will find yourself surrounded by towering pine trees that create a canopy of tranquillity.

    GUNA CAVE

  • GUNA CAVE

    • Guna Cave is also known as "Devil's Kitchen" due to a combination of its dark, mysterious appearance and local folklore surrounding the caves. The name "Devil's Kitchen" evokes a sense of foreboding and intrigue, which is further enhanced by the presence of bat colonies within the caves. Additionally, Hindu mythology suggests the Pandavas used the caves as a kitchen during their exile, contributing to the "kitchen" aspect of the name.

    KODAIKANAL LAKE

  • KODAIKANAL KALE

    • Known as the heart of Kodaikanal, this lake has been a much sought-after location for Bollywood movies. It is an ideal place for you to unwind and get rejuvenated. People can enjoy boating in the lake, either in normal or luxury boats. There are also rowing and pedalling boats.

    COAKERS WALK

  • COAKERS WALK

    • Coaker's Walk is a kilometer-long, paved pedestrian path in Kodaikanal, offering scenic views of the valley below. Built in 1872 by Lt. Coaker, it's a popular spot for nature enthusiasts, photographers, and those seeking a relaxing stroll. The walkway provides panoramic views, especially during sunrise and sunset, and features a telescope house for enhanced viewing.
